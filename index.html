About

I work at the point where academic psychology meets communities it rarely reaches. My master's research examines how collective self-esteem can both protect and burden women facing social stress; my practice ranges from psychiatric hospitals in Lahore to anti-violence workshops in Limerick Prison and adult education with Down Syndrome Limerick. I teach, counsel and interpret across Urdu, Punjabi and English — because good psychology has to speak the language of the people it serves.

Erasmus Mundus scholar (European Commission funded) Member, Psychological Society of Ireland Based in Limerick, Ireland

Research & community projects

Master's research · DAPHNE · 2026

The Double-Edged Resource of Collective Self-Esteem

Problem
Group belonging is treated as a simple protective factor, yet for many women it can also transmit social stress and depressive symptoms.
What I did
Designing and conducting research on the social stress and depressive symptom networks in women, examining collective self-esteem as both resource and risk. Conducted as part of the DAPHNE project.
Outcome
In progress — completing 2026 as my Global Minds master's research project.
BS research · University of the Punjab

A Localized Anxiety Screening Tool for Pakistani Young Adults

Problem
Standard anxiety screening instruments are built and validated on Western populations, limiting their accuracy for young adults in Pakistan.
What I did
Constructed a culturally localized screening tool for anxiety disorders in Pakistani young adults as my BS Honours research project in clinical psychology.
Outcome
Completed as part of a BS Hons awarded with a 3.89/4.00 GPA.
Community initiative · U.S. Dept. of State · 2024

Ink's Link to Joy

Problem
University students carrying anxiety, difficult emotions and trauma often have no structured, low-barrier outlet to process them.
What I did
Led a mental health initiative using expressive writing as a therapeutic tool, helping students steer problematic emotions through guided writing practice.
Outcome
Delivered Jan–Apr 2024 under U.S. Department of State exchange-alumni programming.
Community initiative · University of the Punjab · 2024

Empowering Communities: Wellbeing through Legal Literacy

Problem
Low awareness of legal rights and responsibilities feeds conflict, aggression and stress in diverse communities.
What I did
Co-delivered a seminar programme educating diverse groups on legal rights and responsibilities as a route to wellbeing and reduced aggression.
Outcome
Delivered May–Jul 2024 in association with the University of the Punjab.
